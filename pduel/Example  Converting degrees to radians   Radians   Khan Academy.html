Negative and Positive angles have much to do with the direction the angle opens up.
To elaborate:
An angle is often composed of 2 sides, which are joined by a vertex. The first side, or the starting point, is called the initial side. The second side, or the ending side, is called the terminal side. When constructing an angle on a coordinate plane, with the vertex on zero, the initial side is normally on the X axis. The second side, can be present in any of the quadrants of the coordinate plane. (more) To indicate the direction of the angle, there will most likely be an arrow going from the initial side of the angle to the terminal side. If this arrow is going in a COUNTER-CLOCKWISE direction, then, it is a positive angle. If the arrow is drawn in a CLOCKWISE direction from the initial to terminal side, then the angle is negative.

The radius is the distance from the center of a circle to any point on the perimeter of the circle.
A radian is an central angle that creates an arc with the measure of the radius. It's slightly more useful system to use than degrees. Since there are 2pi radii in a circumference, there are 2pi radians in 360 degrees.

You're right about the way you visualize the definition of a radian. It is "the angle subtended by an arc equal in length to the radius." That is, one radian is the angle you would go through if you went one radius-worth of length on the circle. The radius, however, is a length measurement while the radian is an angle measurement.
